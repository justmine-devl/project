# Untitled Survival Project - Game Design Document

## 1. Game Overview
**Working Title:** Untitled Survival Project
**Genre:** Survival / Exploration / Crafting / Base Building
**Platform:** PC first, later expandable to console
**Camera:** First-person or third-person
**Mode:** Single-player first, with optional co-op or PvP later
**Target Audience:** Players who enjoy tension, resource management, exploration, and progression

## 2. High Concept
The player is dropped into a hostile world where survival is the main challenge. They must manage hunger, thirst, health, stamina, temperature, and limited inventory while gathering resources, crafting tools, building shelter, and surviving threats from the environment, creatures, and possibly other players.

The game is designed around constant pressure, meaningful choices, and long-term progression.

## 3. Design Pillars
- **Survival Comes First**: Every system should support the feeling that the player is fighting to stay alive.
- **Every Resource Matters**: Nothing should feel useless. Food, water, materials, tools, and shelter all have value.
- **Risk and Reward**: Exploration should be dangerous, but the best rewards should be found in the most dangerous places.
- **Player Progression Feels Earned**: The player should start weak and become stronger through effort, planning, and smart decisions.
- **A Harsh World, Not a Fair One**: The world should feel unforgiving, but the game should always remain readable and playable.

## 4. Core Gameplay Loop
1. Explore the world
2. Gather resources
3. Avoid or fight threats
4. Craft tools and survival items
5. Build and defend a base
6. Upgrade the character and unlock new options
7. Push deeper into dangerous zones

## 5. Game Features
### 5.1 Environmental Survival
- Rain and storms, hot/cold regions, forests, deserts, islands, ruined zones.
- Natural dangers: hunger, thirst, disease, exhaustion.

### 5.2 Resource Gathering
- Wood, Stone, Metal, Food, Water, Cloth, Rare materials.

### 5.3 Crafting System
- Tools, Weapons, Armor, Medicine, Cooking items, Building parts, Traps, Utility items.

### 5.4 Survival Mechanics
- Health, Hunger, Thirst, Stamina, Temperature, Sickness/Infection.

### 5.5 Threat System
- Wild animals, Monsters/Zombies, Hostile NPCs, Other players.
- Smart AI that hunts, stalks, or ambushes.

### 5.6 Base Building
- Small shelters, Fortified bases, Storage rooms, Cooking stations, Workbenches, Traps and defenses.

### 5.7 Inventory Management
- Limited inventory to create tension. Decision-making on what to carry/leave/store.

### 5.8 Exploration
- Open areas, Hidden caves, Abandoned structures, Secret loot locations, Rare resource zones, Environmental storytelling.

### 5.9 Story and Lore
- Optional but recommended. Delivered via notes, audio logs, environmental clues, NPC dialogue.

### 5.10 Progression System
- Levels/skill points, unlocking recipes, better tools/weapons, stronger base upgrades, survival bonuses.

## 6. Player Experience Goals
- Feel vulnerable at the start.
- Feel smart when surviving difficult situations.
- Rewarded for preparation.
- Proud of building a strong base.
- Curious about the world.
- Motivated to push further.

## 7. UI / UX Design
### 7.1 HUD
- Health, Hunger, Thirst, Stamina, Temperature indicators.
- Quick inventory slots, interaction prompts.

### 7.2 Inventory Screen
- Item icons, weight/slot count, crafting access, equipment slots.

### 7.3 Crafting Screen
- Available/Locked recipes, required materials, craft time, result preview.

### 7.4 Map and Navigation
- Player position, markers, biomes, points of interest, compass.

### 7.5 Base Interface
- Storing items, repairing structures, building upgrades, activating defenses.

## 8. World Design
### 8.1 Biomes
- Forest, Mountain, Desert, Swamp, Snow, Ruined city, Coastal, Post-apocalyptic.

### 8.2 Points of Interest
- Abandoned houses, military camps, ruins, caves, supply crates, research stations, enemy camps.

### 8.3 World Progression
- From safer, low-value areas to increasingly dangerous zones.

## 9. Combat and Enemy Design
### 9.1 Combat Style
- Simple, tense, tactical: Light/heavy attacks, blocking/dodging, ranged weapons, traps, stealth.

### 9.2 Enemy Types
- Weak wildlife, fast predators, armored creatures, pack hunters, human raiders, boss enemies.

### 9.3 AI Behavior
- Patrol, Search, Chase, Retreat, Group attacks, React to sound/light.

## 10. Progression and Rewards
### 10.1 Skills
- Gathering, Crafting, Combat, Survival, Building, Stealth.

### 10.2 Unlocks
- Better tools, advanced recipes, stronger shelters, efficient harvesting, improved combat.

### 10.3 Long-Term Goals
- Secure base, survive harsh season, reach hidden region, defeat major enemy, solve world mystery.

## 11. Game Modes
- Single-Player: Focused on survival and story.
- Co-op: Collaborative resource gathering and defense.
- PvP: Competitive raiding and fighting.

## 12. Art Direction
- Mood: Realistic/gritty or Stylized/dark.
- Priorities: Readability, weather effects, distinct biomes, wear and damage.

## 13. Audio Direction
- Environmental ambience, weather, footsteps, crafting, enemy cues, alerts, structure sounds.
- Subtle, tension-based music.

## 14. Development Tasks
### Phase 1: Prototype
- Player movement
- Resource gathering
- Hunger and thirst
- Simple crafting
- One basic enemy
- Simple shelter building

### Phase 2: Core Systems
- Health, stamina, temperature
- Inventory limits
- More crafting recipes
- More enemy behaviors
- Day/night cycle
- Saving and loading

### Phase 3: World and Content
- Multiple biomes
- Exploration locations
- Loot tables
- Base upgrades
- More items and tools
- Environmental hazards

### Phase 4: Progression and Story
- Skill system
- Unlockable recipes
- Quest structure
- Lore items
- Story progression
- Mid/late game content

### Phase 5: Polish
- UI clarity, Balance, Enemy AI, Performance, Audio, Visual effects, Bug fixes.

## 15. MVP Scope
- One map
- One player character
- Hunger, thirst, health
- Basic gathering
- Basic crafting
- Basic inventory
- One shelter type
- One enemy type
- Save/load system

## 16. Success Criteria
- Quick understanding of survival systems.
- Tension without confusion.
- Enjoyment of exploration.
- Care for base building.
- Reward for surviving harsh conditions.
- Motivation for progress.

## 17. Final Vision
A survival experience where every decision matters, demanding alertness, resource management, and adaptation in a world that constantly pushes back.
